Geospatial Analyst

My interest in geospatial analysis began at Northern Arizona University where I studied remote sensing and geographic information systems (GIS). School projects included using Satellite imagery and aerial photography of geologic feature such as lava flows and faults near the Little Colorado River. In addition, I studied pre-historic Native American sites, including the Sinagua people group. Imagery used in these studies included multi-spectral, hyper-spectral, and synthetic aperture radar. GIS and Remote Sensing software used included Erdas Imagine and ESRI products such as ArcView and ArcInfo. After NAU, I spent 25 years working as a geospatial analyst at the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA) using more ESRI products including the ESRI web API, ArcGIS Server & Engine, ArcGIS online, and ArcGIS Pro. Through NGA, I received my MS in GIS with the University of Redlands and presented my thesis project at the ESRI Users Conference in 2005.

Imagery Analyst

Although my official title at the National Geospatial -Intelligence Agency was geospatial analysts, I often worked as an imagery analyst in support of the Department of War(formerly Defense) and the Intelligence Community (IC). My duties included exploiting national and commercial imagery to answer key intelligence questions (KIQs). Job duties included using Remote View, SocetSet GXP, and ISPY Electronic Light Table (ELT) software to review imagery and publish official U.S. government reports and cables. I worked issues including wars in Afghanistan and the Ukraine, as well as natural disasters including earthquakes, floods, and typhoons. I retired from NGA in 2025.
